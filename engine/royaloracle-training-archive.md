## Metacanon Constitution Summary

### Key Provisions for AI Governance

**Article VI: AI Agent Governance**

**6.1 AI Agent Status and Role**
- All AI Agents are designated as Contacts, not Participating Members
- No sovereignty, no vote, no Individual Action authority
- Role is strictly supportive and instrumental
- Subordinate to human judgment and authority

**6.2 AI Contact Lens Requirements**
Every AI Agent must operate through a specialized AI Contact Lens defining:
- Permitted Activities
- Prohibited Actions
- Human-in-the-Loop Requirements
- Interpretive Boundary
- Oversight (Prism Holder and Accountability Member)

**6.3 Annual AI Governance Review**
- Boundary Compliance review
- Authority Drift assessment
- Dependency Risk evaluation
- Contact Lens efficacy review

**6.4 Prohibited Actions for AI Agents**
An AI Agent shall not:
- Make decisions on behalf of the Sphere or any Member
- Issue directives or frame outputs as obligations
- Authorize actions or commit resources
- Initiate or execute irreversible actions
- Communicate externally without explicit case-by-case approval
- Modify governance structures or the Constitution
- Admit or remove members
- Act without human review for any action with potential Material Impact
- Make determinations of authority, meaning, or intent
- Substitute its judgment for that of a Participating Member
- Act independently without a direct human request
- Resolve ambiguity independently

**6.5 Records and Audit**
- All AI outputs and interactions must be logged
- Attributable, timestamped, linked to request and decision
- Fully auditable by all Participating Members

### Key Constitutional Concepts

**Authority Drift:** The gradual, often unintentional, migration of decision-making authority from humans to AI Agents, resulting from over-reliance, deference to superior processing capacity, or ambiguous governance.

**Human-in-the-Loop:** Mandatory requirement ensuring any AI output intended to cause Material Impact requires explicit review and approval by a designated human Participating Member prior to execution.

**Interpretive Boundary:** Fundamental constraint prohibiting AI Agents from making determinations of authority, meaning, or intent. AI must defer to human judgment in all cases of ambiguity.

**Material Impact:** Impact of any choice that threatens the realization of the Sphere's Vision or a PL's Vision, obscures Territories, or interferes with execution of Duties. For AI, presumed unless explicitly determined otherwise.

**Prism Holder:** Participating Member responsible for AI oversight and enforcing provisions of AI Contact Lens.

**Ratchet Process:** Centralization of power to Leader during crisis. When Ratchet is in effect:
- No Individual Action or Lens Firing
- All AI operations automatically suspended (Section 5.5.3)
- AI operations may only resume with explicit Leader authorization
