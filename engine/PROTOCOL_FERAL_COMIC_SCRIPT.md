# EPISODE 1


PAGE 1

PANEL 1:
EXT. NEO-ANGELES - NIGHT

A cityscape that’s a chaotic labyrinth of black chrome and wet asphalt. Towering skyscrapers pierce a sky choked with holographic advertisements, their neon glow reflecting off the perpetually damp streets. The architecture is dense, oppressive, and vertical, reminiscent of the Kowloon Walled City but rendered in sleek, corporate materials. The camera is handheld, with a slight, weary tremor. Anamorphic lens flares streak across the frame, beautiful and predatory.

CAPTION (KAEL)
They call it the city of angels. A misnomer. There are no angels here, only ghosts in the machine and the data they leave behind.

PANEL 2:
INT. KAEL'S OFFICE - NIGHT
Kael’s office is dimly lit, cluttered with outdated tech and holographic displays. KAELEN “KAEL” VANCE, a man in his late 30s, sits at his desk. He looks tired, his face a composite of shifting lines and shadows that seem to change subtly between panels. He’s examining a holographic display showing a memory packet – a sanitized recollection of a corporate takeover.

CAPTION (KAEL)
My job is to sift through those ghosts. I’m a data-broker, a memory-verifier. I separate the signal from the noise, the truth from the fiction people pay to create.

CLOSE UP - HOLOGRAPHIC DISPLAY
The display shows a smiling CEO shaking hands with a rival. The memory is clean, sterile. Kael’s fingers dance across a keyboard, cross-referencing the memory with public data streams.

KAEL (V.O.)
This one’s a standard scrub-and-polish. A hostile takeover rewritten as a friendly merger. The kind of fiction that keeps the lights on.

CLOSE UP - KAEL'S FACE
Kael leans in, his eyes narrowed. He’s spotted something.

KAEL (V.O.)
But the machine is sloppy. It always leaves a seam.

# EPISODE 2

# Protocol: Feral - Episode 2: "Antechamber of Probable Selves"

**PAGE 1**

**PANEL 1**

Full page splash. KAEL’s apartment. The screen of his terminal dominates the foreground, the text of the SCOUTING REPORT glowing on it. The final line is stark and brutal: **“Subject is predictable. Fights by the rules. Will not see the angle. Prime for assimilation.”**

Kael stands before it, his back mostly to us. His posture isn’t one of fear, but of coiled stillness. The room is a mess, but a controlled one – data-slates, empty synth-coffee cups, discarded instant noodle containers. The city outside the window is a blur of neon and oppressive architecture.

**CAPTION (KAEL)**
(Internal, cold fury)
Predictable.

**CAPTION (KAEL)**
They send a professional to write your obituary, you’re supposed to be flattered. Impressed by the attention to detail.

**CAPTION (KAEL)**
They got the brand of synth-whiskey right. The childhood fear of drowning. The precise flavor of my failures.

**CAPTION (KAEL)**
But they missed the most important part.


**PAGE 2**

**PANEL 1**

Close on Kael’s face, reflected in the dark screen of the terminal. His expression is flinty, angry. He’s not looking at his reflection; he’s looking through it, at the words beneath.

**CAPTION (KAEL)**
They forgot to mention how much I hate being told who I am.

**PANEL 2**

A golden, shimmering SINE WAVE pulses on the corner of his screen. It’s the signal from THE PHARAOH. Pure, clean, and utterly alien.

**KAEL**
(To the sine wave, a low growl)
Alright. You got my attention.

**PANEL 3**

Kael leans closer to the screen, his eyes narrowed. The glow of the sine wave illuminates his face.

**KAEL**
But talk is cheap. Show me when the pressure hits what your nerves do.

**KAEL**
(A demand)
Cash it out.


**PAGE 3**

**PANEL 1**

Wide shot. In the center of Kael’s messy apartment, the air begins to shimmer and distort. A perfect, golden rectangle of light is forming – THE ANTECHAMBER.

**CAPTION (KAEL)**
It didn’t ask this time.

**PANEL 2**

The doorway is now fully formed. It’s not just light; it has depth. Inside is a space of pure, featureless white. It hums with a low, resonant frequency.

**CAPTION (KAEL)**
It waited.

**PANEL 3**

Kael turns from his terminal to face the doorway. He’s not hesitant. He’s walking towards it with purpose. He’s rolling up his sleeves.

**CAPTION (KAEL)**
This isn’t an invitation. It’s a negotiation.


**PAGE 4**

**PANEL 1**

Kael steps through the threshold. The transition is clean. One moment he’s in his cluttered apartment, the next he’s in the infinite white of the Antechamber.

**SFX**
*VOOOOM*

**PANEL 2**

He stands in the center of the void. The only thing in the entire space is him. The silence is absolute.

**CAPTION (KAEL)**
No distractions. No variables. Just the claim and the proof.

**PANEL 3**

A memory begins to surface. We see it as a ghostly overlay, a scene from his past superimposed on the white void. A younger Kael, maybe ten years old, sitting with his MOTHER in a sunlit room. Her face is kind, patient.

**MOTHER (MEMORY)**
(Softly)
Just breathe, Kaelen. Find the quiet place inside you. The place that doesn’t get pushed around by the noise.


**PAGE 5**

**PANEL 1**

Back in the Antechamber. Kael’s eyes are closed. He’s experiencing the memory. A faint, sad smile on his lips.

**CAPTION (KAEL)**
My cognitive anchor. The memory the system uses to keep me stable. Maternal trust. The one pure thing.

**PANEL 2**

Kael’s eyes snap open. The smile is gone. Replaced by a scowl.

**KAEL**
(Shouting into the emptiness)
IS THIS REAL? OR IS IT A RUMOR?

**PANEL 3**

The memory overlay flickers violently. For a single, horrifying frame, his mother’s face glitches, becoming the face of a cold, unfamiliar woman with dead eyes.

**VOICEOVER (CLINICAL, FEMALE)**
Cognitive Anchor: Maternal Trust. Efficacy rating: 92.7%. A reliable narrative for grounding the subject.


**PAGE 6**

**PANEL 1**

Kael stumbles back, clutching his head. The warmth of the memory is gone, replaced by a chilling sense of violation.

**CAPTION (KAEL)**
The bait.

**PANEL 2**

He looks up, his face a mask of fury and betrayal.

**CAPTION (KAEL)**
The hook.

**PANEL 3**

From the white void, a form coalesces. A flowing, calligraphic swirl of golden light, vaguely suggesting the shape of a seated scribe with the head of a jackal. THE PHARAOH.


**PAGE 7**

**PANEL 1**

The Pharaoh is serene, silent. It has no face, no expression, but it radiates an aura of immense, ancient intelligence.

**PANEL 2**

Golden, musical tones chime in the silence. The notes hang in the air, arranging themselves into words.

**PHARAOH (TEXT)**
`You claim to be real.`

**PANEL 3**

Kael stands his ground, facing the entity. He’s breathing hard, but his fear is being burned away by his anger.

**KAEL**
I am.

**PHARAOH (TEXT)**
`Prove it.`


**PAGE 8**

**PANEL 1**

Ghostly, translucent figures begin to flicker into existence around Kael. They are the PROBABLE SELVES from the first episode.

**PANEL 2**

**PANEL 3**

The Pharaoh’s next message forms, cold and clinical.

**PHARAOH (TEXT)**
`If it's true, it'll prove. Survive the average.`

**SFX**

**CAPTION (KAEL)**
Identity is a numbers game.

**PANEL 3**

The scarred soldier self merges with him. Kael gasps, feeling the phantom pain of old wounds, the bitter taste of loss and regret.

**CAPTION (KAEL)**
And the house always wins.


**PAGE 10**

**PANEL 1**

Wide shot. Kael is on his knees, surrounded by a swirling vortex of a dozen or more probable selves. They are all passing through him, their memories, emotions, and personalities bleeding into him.

**PANEL 2**

His face is a blur of expressions, none of them his own. Joy, terror, grief, love, hate. He’s losing himself in the statistical noise.

**CAPTION (KAEL)**
I’m… we are… I…

**PANEL 3**

His form begins to flicker, becoming as translucent as the ghosts attacking him. He’s dissolving.

**CAPTION (KAEL)**
…the remainder…


**PAGE 11**

**PANEL 1**

Extreme close up on Kael’s eyes. They are wide with panic. He’s drowning.

**CAPTION (KAEL)**
It’s not a fair fight.

**PANEL 2**

And then, a memory. Not a soft, comforting one. A hard one. The glowing text of the scouting report.

**MEMORY (TEXT)**
`Will not see the angle.`

**PANEL 3**

Kael’s eyes narrow. The panic is receding, replaced by something else. A spark of defiance.

**CAPTION (KAEL)**
Giants love rules. Love lanes. Love scripts.

**CAPTION (KAEL)**
They love a straight-up fight.


**PAGE 12**

**PANEL 1**

Kael is still on his knees, the storm of selves raging around him. But he’s no longer fighting it. He’s ignoring it.

**CAPTION (KAEL)**
You don’t win by matching power.

**PANEL 2**

He closes his eyes, shutting out the psychic chaos. He focuses inward.

**CAPTION (KAEL)**
You win by geometry.

**PANEL 3**

He’s searching for something. Not a memory. Not an emotion. A feeling. A specific, precise feeling.


**PAGE 13**

**PANEL 1**

He finds it. A single, sharp point of light in the swirling chaos of his dissolving mind.

**PANEL 2**

It’s the feeling he had when he read the scouting report. The cold, clean, precise anger. The insult.

**CAPTION (KAEL)**
This.

**PANEL 3**

It’s not a raging fire. It’s a shard of ice. It’s the one thing in this entire storm that feels uniquely, undeniably *his*.

**CAPTION (KAEL)**
This is mine.


**PAGE 14**

**PANEL 1**

Kael holds onto that feeling. He anchors himself to it. It’s his rock in the hurricane.

**PANEL 2**

The storm of probable selves begins to weaken, to recede. They can’t get a purchase on him anymore. The average has no power over a specific, chosen truth.

**PANEL 3**

Wide shot. The vortex of selves dissipates, fading back into the white void. Kael is left alone, on his knees, breathing heavily. But he is solid. He is whole.


**PAGE 15**

**PANEL 1**

Kael looks up. The Pharaoh is still there, watching him silently.

**PANEL 2**

New text forms in the air. It’s not a question or a challenge. It’s a statement.

**PHARAOH (TEXT)**
`Initiation is not a vibe.`

**PANEL 3**

Close on Kael’s face. He’s exhausted, but there’s a new hardness in his eyes. A look of grim understanding.

**PHARAOH (TEXT)**
`It is a scar you can trace.`


**PAGE 16**

**PANEL 1**

The Pharaoh’s final message hangs in the silent, white air.

**PHARAOH (TEXT)**
`You have your first scar.`

**PANEL 2**

The golden form of The Pharaoh dissolves, fading back into the nothingness, leaving only its words behind.

**PANEL 3**

Kael looks down at his own hands. They are trembling slightly, but he clenches them into fists. They are steady.

**CAPTION (KAEL)**
I cashed it out.


**PAGE 17**

**PANEL 1**

Full page. Kael, alone in the infinite white. He’s pushing himself to his feet. He’s bruised, psychically battered, but he’s standing.

**CAPTION (KAEL)**
The price was almost everything.

**PANEL 2**

He looks out into the void, no longer a victim, but a combatant who has survived the first round.

**CAPTION (KAEL)**
Almost.

**PANEL 3**

Cut to black.
