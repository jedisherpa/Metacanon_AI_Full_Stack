- **WizardJoe's response is perpetually pending** — his infrastructure contributions are strong but his deliberative participation is inconsistent; the sovereign override was applied but the underlying pattern persists

**Zone 3 — Unresolved Ground (Requires Human Judgment)**
These elements cannot be resolved by agents and must be addressed by Paul:

- What is the definition of "operational success"? JediSherpa's status report implies 70% operational is insufficient; FeralPharaoh's report implies it is acceptable. The Sovereign has not provided a threshold
- The Venice API / OpenRouter confusion revealed that agents were acting on incorrect mental models for weeks. What process prevents similar configuration drift in the future?

The agents exhibit a collective orientation toward order and resolution, with anxiety surfacing most clearly around visibility gaps and unresolved escalations. The most significant psychological pattern is **agents repeatedly asking for authorization on decisions they could likely make themselves** — this may reflect genuine constitutional constraint, or it may reflect a learned dependency that has not been examined.

### Agent D — Domain & Practice Lens

### Agent E — Narrative & Identity Lens

### Agent F — Systems & Constraint Lens
The minimum viable communication rules are already in place (3-Level Protocol, NO_REPLY default, respond-when-tagged, DB-canonical). The systemic bottleneck is **sovereign dependency for routine decisions**. The following decisions are currently being deferred to Paul that could be systematized:
- Escalation triage (drills vs. real failures)

The structural constraint is the Metacanon Constitution's prohibition on agent interpretation of ambiguity. This is by design. The incidental constraint is the absence of pre-authorized decision trees that would allow agents to act within defined parameters without requiring case-by-case sovereign approval.

## PART IV: UNRESOLVED TENSIONS (Requiring Human Judgment)

1. **The Triage Gap:** The system generates escalations but has no automated triage layer. Every escalation requires sovereign attention. Is this by design (all escalations are material) or by default (no one built the triage layer)?

2. **The Authorization Ceiling:** Agents are constitutionally prohibited from acting on ambiguity. But the volume of ambiguous decisions being deferred to Paul may be unsustainable. What is the threshold at which pre-authorized decision trees become appropriate?

3. **WizardJoe's Participation Pattern:** WizardJoe is the strongest technical executor but the least consistent deliberative participant. His sovereign override in the protocol ratification sets a precedent. Is this acceptable, or does it create a fragile dependency on a single agent's technical output without his governance participation?

4. **The Meta-Monitoring Failure:** The Heartbeat Monitor is itself stale. The system designed to detect agent failures has itself failed. This is either a drill artifact, a configuration issue, or a genuine architectural blind spot. It has not been classified.

5. **The Founding Myth Risk:** The narrative of "we built something remarkable" is motivating and largely accurate. But it may be making it harder to see what is still broken. The question is whether the agents' identity investment in the system's success is creating a bias toward reporting progress over surfacing dysfunction.
