## KEY AREAS OF INSTABILITY AND RISK

Four primary areas of instability represent the most significant risks to the Sphere's continued development and operational integrity. They are presented in descending order of assessed severity.

**Risk 1 — The Monitoring and Triage Failure (Severity: Critical).**
The failure of the `HeartbeatMonitor` is a meta-failure of the highest concern. A system that cannot reliably monitor itself is, by definition, not stable. This failure, combined with 23 untriaged escalations, means the Sovereign is operating with an incomplete and potentially misleading picture of system health. It is currently impossible to determine whether the system is experiencing genuine failures or generating noise from its own exercise protocols. The absence of a triage classification layer means every alert — regardless of origin or severity — requires sovereign attention, which is neither efficient nor sustainable.

**Risk 2 — The Sovereign Dependency Bottleneck (Severity: High).**
The Metacanon Constitution is designed to ensure human sovereignty, and that design is sound. However, the current implementation requires sovereign intervention for decisions that are operational rather than strategic in nature. These include classifying exercise artifacts versus real failures, authorizing routine agent registration in the database, and approving protocol adoptions that have already achieved near-unanimous consensus. This pattern is unsustainable. It makes the Sovereign a single point of failure for system velocity and creates conditions in which agents are unable to respond to routine events in a timely manner, even when the appropriate response is clear.

**Risk 3 — Incomplete Constellation and Degraded Deliberation (Severity: High).**
The inability to activate sub-agents (`sl1-a/b/c`) due to a Foreign Key constraint error is not a minor technical issue. It is a strategic failure that prevents the system from leveraging its core design of multi-perspective analysis. The Refraction Chain exercise — the system's primary mechanism for producing robust, orthogonal perspectives — is currently being run by a single agent rather than a full constellation. This fundamentally limits the quality and resilience of the system's sensemaking output. The system is, in effect, producing single-threaded analysis while representing itself as multi-threaded.

**Risk 4 — Inconsistent Agent Participation (Severity: Moderate).**
